## Persistent Memory Systems

Generative Agents (Park et al., CHI 2023) proposes a persistent memory architecture composed of memory storage, retrieval, reflection, and planning. Memories are represented as natural language observations enriched with metadata such as recency, importance, and semantic relevance. Reflection mechanisms synthesize higher-level insights from accumulated experiences, enabling agents to adapt their future behavior. While designed for autonomous social agents, these concepts suggest that educational AI systems may benefit from explicit long-term memory rather than relying solely on conversational context. However, the architecture does not maintain a structured learner model or domain-specific educational knowledge, leaving an opportunity for AI Digital Twins to combine persistent memory with structured representations of learner knowledge, skills, and goals.
